Long before the age of struggle, the world was governed by the **Greater Will**, an outer cosmic force that imposed order through the **Elden Ring**, a metaphysical law embedded within the **Erdtree**. The Elden Ring defined reality itself: life, death, causality, and hierarchy. Its bearer was **Queen Marika the Eternal**, a Numen chosen as a god and ruler of the **Lands Between**.

Marika ruled alongside her consort **Godfrey**, the first Elden Lord. Godfrey was a warrior king who conquered Marika’s enemies, including the giants and other ancient powers, establishing the Golden Order. When all worthy foes were defeated, Godfrey lost his grace. Marika stripped him and his warriors of divine favor and exiled them. These exiles became the **Tarnished**, sent beyond the Lands Between with a prophecy that one day they would return.

After Godfrey’s exile, Marika took a new consort, **Radagon**, a red haired champion who had once warred against her enemies and later ruled the land of Liurnia. Radagon and Marika were later revealed to be two aspects of the same being, a single god divided.

Marika and Radagon had several children, demigods who inherited fragments of divine power. Among them were **Malenia**, cursed with Scarlet Rot, and **Miquella**, cursed with eternal youth. From earlier unions came others such as **Godwyn the Golden**, beloved and noble, **Radahn**, a mighty general who held back the stars, **Rykard**, who sought forbidden power, and **Ranni**, an empyrean chosen as a potential successor to Marika.

The first great catastrophe occurred during the **Night of the Black Knives**. A group of assassins wielding fragments of the **Rune of Death** murdered Godwyn’s soul, making him the first demigod to truly die. At the same time, Ranni orchestrated her own death in body, freeing her soul from the Greater Will’s control. Godwyn’s corrupted corpse became the source of deathroot, spreading undeath across the land.

Driven by grief, rage, or defiance, Queen Marika shattered the Elden Ring itself. This act broke the Golden Order and destabilized reality. As punishment, the Greater Will imprisoned Marika within the Erdtree. Radagon attempted to repair the Ring but failed, and he too was bound within the tree.

With the Elden Ring broken, its fragments, the **Great Runes**, were claimed by the demigods. This led to a brutal civil war known as **the Shattering**. The demigods fought endlessly for supremacy, but none could claim true victory. Radahn halted the movement of the stars to prevent fate itself from progressing. Malenia marched to stop him, and their battle devastated Caelid, leaving Radahn mindless and the land poisoned by rot. Rykard rejected the Golden Order entirely and allowed himself to be consumed by a blasphemous serpent. Others retreated into madness, stagnation, or schemes of their own.

As the world decayed and grace faded, the Greater Will lodestoneed the Tarnished from exile. Grace once again guided them toward the Lands Between, not out of mercy, but desperation. The demigods had failed, and a new Elden Lord was needed.

The Tarnished travels the broken land, slaying demigods and claiming their Great Runes, uncovering the truth behind the Golden Order’s lies. They learn that the Greater Will is not benevolent, that the Erdtree is a tool of control, and that alternatives to its rule exist.

At the heart of the Erdtree, the Tarnished confronts Radagon and the **Elden Beast**, the living incarnation of the Greater Will’s law. With their defeat, the Tarnished stands at a crossroads.

Several endings are possible, each reflecting a different philosophy. The Tarnished may restore the Golden Order in altered form, perpetuating divine rule. They may embrace **Ranni’s vision**, rejecting the Greater Will entirely and ushering in an Age of Stars, where fate is freed from cosmic control. They may choose despair, chaos, or the Frenzied Flame, burning the world to escape all order and suffering.

Elden Ring’s story is ultimately about power imposed from above, rebellion against divine authority, and the cost of order versus freedom. The world is not saved. It is reshaped according to the will of the one who endures.
