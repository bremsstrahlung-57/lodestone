RAG, or Retrieval-Augmented Generation, is a framework that combines the strengths of retrieval-based and generative models. It enhances the capabilities of language models by allowing them to access external knowledge sources during the generation process. 

In a typical RAG setup, a model retrieves relevant documents or information from a knowledge base and uses this information to inform its responses. This approach helps in generating more accurate and contextually relevant outputs, especially in scenarios where the model's training data may not cover specific topics comprehensively.

RAG can be particularly useful in applications such as question answering, chatbots, and content generation, where up-to-date and precise information is crucial. By integrating retrieval mechanisms, RAG models can provide richer and more informative interactions, bridging the gap between static knowledge and dynamic user queries.
